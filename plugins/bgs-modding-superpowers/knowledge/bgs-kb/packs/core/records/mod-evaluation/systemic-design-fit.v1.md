# A good modpack mod reinforces the game's systemic-simulation design

Treat Bethesda games as systemic simulators, not stage-managed scripts. A mod earns its slot when it strengthens the world model the pack is trying to express: actions leave traces, rules propagate through state, and player choices produce feedback the game can remember.

Freedom is not the number of menu choices a mod adds. It is how much meaningful state the player can leave behind, and whether the surrounding world can respond to that state. Immersion is not cinematic intensity; it is feedback consistency and behavior that remains traceable after the moment passes.

This makes modding a form of co-authorship rather than a patch list. The curator is not only asking whether a mod is impressive in isolation, but whether it belongs inside the pack's declared 风格.

Stability is the floor: an unstable pack fails before judgment can begin. 风格 is the soul: without a declared style, there is no axis for deciding whether a mod reinforces the pack or merely adds noise.
